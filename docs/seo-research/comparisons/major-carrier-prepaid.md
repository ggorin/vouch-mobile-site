# Major Carrier Prepaid Plans Research

## Overview
Research on prepaid phone plans from AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile for 2024-2025. This document compiles current pricing, features, and comparisons to help understand the prepaid market landscape and competitive positioning.

---

## 1. AT&T Prepaid

### Current Plans and Pricing

#### Monthly Plans
- **$15 Monthly Plan**: Unlimited talk/text, 2GB high-speed data
- **8GB Plan**: $33/month (or $99 for 3 months); unlimited talk/text, 8GB data with HD streaming capability
- **16GB Plan**: $25/month; unlimited talk/text, 16GB data, 10GB hotspot data
- **Unlimited Plan**: $45/month with 30GB cap at 3 Mbps speeds
- **Unlimited Max**: $55/month; unlimited data with 25GB hotspot data and 50GB high-speed data cap
- **Unlimited Max Plus**: $65/month; no data cap on high-speed data, 35GB hotspot data

#### Annual Plans
- **Unlimited Annual**: $240 for 12 months (unlimited talk/text/data, 10GB hotspot)
- **16GB Annual**: $300 for 12 months ($25/month equivalent; 16GB monthly data, 10GB hotspot)

#### Key Features
- 5G access on all modern plans
- Unlimited talk and text in US, Mexico, and Canada
- International texting to 230+ countries
- **Data Rollover**: Available on select plans
- **AutoPay Discount**: $10/month savings when enrolled
- **Level Up Feature**: Use payment history to upgrade to postpaid with $0 down, regardless of credit score

### Network Priority vs Postpaid
- **QCI Priority Levels**: AT&T uses Quality of Service Class Indicators (QCI) to manage network priority
  - QCI 7: AT&T Turbo postpaid customers (highest priority)
  - QCI 8: Standard postpaid accounts
  - QCI 9: Most AT&T prepaid plans (matching entry-level postpaid)

- **Prepaid Data Performance**: Standard prepaid data is not inherently slower, but prepaid plans typically have smaller premium high-speed data allotments than postpaid counterparts, leading to faster deprioritization during congestion

- **Turbo Add-On (New 2024)**: Prepaid customers on Unlimited Max plan can purchase $7/month "Turbo" add-on for QCI 7 priority, giving them same high priority as premium postpaid customers

### Pros
- Wide range of plan options from budget-friendly to unlimited
- Excellent data rollover options
- Significant AutoPay discounts ($10/month)
- Strong international features (200+ countries)
- Level Up pathway to postpaid credit building
- New Turbo option for prepaid customers wanting high priority
- 5G access across most plans

### Cons
- Lower base network priority than standard postpaid (unless paying for Turbo)
- Entry-level plans have limited data caps
- Limited hotspot data on mid-tier plans (25GB-35GB max)
- Need Turbo add-on to match postpaid priority
- Higher plan entry price ($15 minimum) compared to some competitors

## 2. Verizon Prepaid

### Current Plans and Pricing

#### Monthly Plans
- **Talk & Text Only**: $35/month (or $30/month with AutoPay); unlimited talk/text, 2G speeds only, no hotspot
- **15GB Plan**: $45/month (or $35/month with AutoPay); 15GB 4G/5G speeds, share hotspot from allowance then 2G speeds
- **Unlimited Plan**: $60/month (or $50/month with AutoPay); unlimited data at 4G/5G speeds (DVD-quality streaming up to 480p), 5GB premium hotspot then reduced speeds
- **Unlimited Plus**: $70/month (or $60/month with AutoPay); 50GB premium data then unlimited lower-speed data, 25GB premium hotspot then 3 Mbps on 5G Ultra Wideband, includes Global Choice (1 country included)

#### Special Features
- **Loyalty Discounts**: Additional $5/month after 3 months, another $5/month after 9 months (total $10/month savings on plans $45+)
- **5G Access**: Available on 15GB and higher plans, with 5G Ultra Wideband on Unlimited Plus
- **International**: Mexico/Canada included on most plans; Global Choice on Unlimited Plus

### Network Priority vs Postpaid
- **Prepaid Deprioritization**: Main difference from postpaid is network priority/throughput
  - Postpaid: Gets "fast lane" priority during congestion
  - Prepaid: Deprioritized first when network is congested, may experience slower speeds in busy urban areas

- **Premium Prepaid Exception**: Verizon's Unlimited Plus and Unlimited Ultimate prepaid packages offer truly unlimited priority data that doesn't slow down regardless of usage, providing comparable performance to postpaid

- **Practical Impact**: While standard prepaid plans experience deprioritization during congestion, premium tiers can match postpaid performance

- **3-Year Price Lock Guarantee**: All prepaid plans include price protection on base rates

### Pros
- Highest quality 5G network coverage
- Competitive pricing with strong AutoPay discounts ($10-15/month savings)
- Premium prepaid options (Unlimited Plus) offer comparable priority to postpaid
- Loyalty discounts reward long-term customers
- No contracts or long-term commitments
- 5G Ultra Wideband available on higher tiers
- Price lock guarantee provides budget certainty

### Cons
- Talk & Text plan limited to 2G speeds (slowest tier)
- Standard prepaid plans deprioritized during network congestion
- Streaming limited to DVD quality (480p) on Unlimited plan
- Higher pricing compared to T-Mobile (base Unlimited $60 vs T-Mobile $50)
- Hotspot data more limited (5GB premium on Unlimited vs T-Mobile's unlimited)
- Limited device selection compared to postpaid

## 3. T-Mobile Prepaid (Connect & Simply Prepaid)

### Current Plans and Pricing

#### Connect by T-Mobile (Budget Brand)
- **5GB Plan**: $15/month; unlimited talk/text, 5GB high-speed data

#### Standard T-Mobile Prepaid Monthly Plans
- **Starter Monthly**: $45/month (or $40/month with AutoPay); 15GB premium data then unlimited 2G, shareable hotspot data
- **Unlimited Monthly**: $50/month (or $45/month with AutoPay); unlimited 5G data with 50GB premium data, unlimited hotspot with 3G speeds
- **Unlimited Plus Monthly**: $65/month (or $60/month with AutoPay); unlimited 5G with 50GB premium, 5GB high-speed hotspot, unlimited international texting to 215+ countries

#### Multi-Line Plans
- **2 for $75/month**: Unlimited 5G data, 3GB hotspot per line, includes Canada/Mexico calling/texting
- **4 for $100/month**: 10GB 5G data per line, shareable hotspot, unlimited talk/text

#### Simply Prepaid Plans
- **Multi-line option**: 4 lines for $100 (10GB 4G LTE data per phone), 5th line $30

### Network Priority vs Postpaid
- **High Priority for T-Mobile Prepaid**: Unlike AT&T and Verizon, most T-Mobile postpaid and prepaid branded plans receive high priority

- **Premium Data Priority**: T-Mobile prepaid premium/priority data has the SAME priority level as postpaid customers—it is NOT deprioritized during network congestion

- **Competitive Advantage**: T-Mobile's prepaid plans perform very competitively because their premium data receives same prioritization as postpaid, making them unique among major carriers

- **Data Caps Difference**: Metro by T-Mobile (prepaid subsidiary) gives 35GB of full-speed data before throttling; postpaid gets 50GB

- **General Pattern**: Postpaid > T-Mobile prepaid > Prepaid from MVNOs

- **5-Year Price Guarantee**: T-Mobile prepaid includes price lock on talk, text, and 5G smartphone data for 5 years on eligible plans

### Pros
- **Most competitive pricing** among major carriers (Unlimited $50 vs Verizon $60)
- **Premium data same priority as postpaid** (unique among Big 3)
- **Strong multi-line pricing** ($2.50/line for 4+ lines at $100)
- Excellent international features (Unlimited Plus: 215+ countries, US/Mexico/Canada unlimited)
- No deprioritization of premium data during congestion
- 5-year price guarantee for budget certainty
- T-Mobile Tuesdays perks on all plans
- Scam Block security feature
- Very affordable entry option ($15 Connect plan)

### Cons
- Starter plan limited to 15GB before unlimited 2G kicks in
- Limited hotspot (3GB on multi-line, 5GB on Unlimited Plus)
- Multi-line plans require specific line counts for best pricing
- Connect (budget brand) may not include all features of standard prepaid
- Less device selection for prepaid customers

## Why Customers Choose Prepaid Alternatives

### Primary Drivers
1. **Cost Savings**: Most prepaid customers save 50% or more vs. major carriers
2. **No Long-Term Contracts**: Freedom to change plans monthly without penalties
3. **No Credit Checks**: Accessible for those with poor/no credit history
4. **Budget Control**: Fixed monthly costs with no surprise overage charges
5. **Flexibility**: Ability to upgrade/downgrade based on monthly needs
6. **Transparency**: Simple pricing with no hidden fees or surcharges

### Ideal Prepaid Customers
- Budget-conscious users
- Occasional users with variable needs
- Those with poor or no credit history
- People avoiding long-term commitments
- Customers wanting month-to-month flexibility
- Secondary phone users
- International travelers (looking for flexibility)

---

## Cons of Prepaid vs Postpaid

### Network & Speed Issues
- Standard prepaid plans experience deprioritization during network congestion
- Limited data allowances compared to many postpaid plans
- Streaming quality capped on some plans (Verizon: 480p max)

### Service Limitations
- Most plans don't allow data rollover (except AT&T's rollover plans)
- Limited phone selection for prepaid customers
- Limited hotspot data (except T-Mobile)
- No device subsidies/financing options
- Must pay full phone price upfront

### Feature Gaps
- Fewer frills and perks than postpaid
- No bundling discounts with other services
- Limited family plan options

## Key Takeaways for Vouch Positioning

### Competitive Strengths to Emphasize
1. **T-Mobile Prepaid**: Best value with premium data priority matching postpaid—but Vouch competes by being MVNO (more affordable than even T-Mobile prepaid)
2. **AT&T Prepaid**: Strong data rollover and international features, Turbo option for power users
